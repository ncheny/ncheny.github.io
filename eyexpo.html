Eyexpo

Graphic design and 3D asset building for VR
Eyexpo specializes in virtual reality (VR) content production and VR creation developmemt for clients. While working here, I created graphic designs, managed their social media accounts, and created 2D/3D assets for their Unity VR developments.
  • TASKS Research, conceptualization, advertisement graphic design, social media management, architectural 3D environment building, Photoshop rendering
  • TOOLS Illustrator, Photoshop, Maya, Sketchup, Unity 3D
  • CO-OP
  • SUMMER 2017 5 Month

Eyexpo Company Website

Graphic Designs

I worked closely with the design lead and the advertising manager in creating and tweaking the designs to reflect the company’s image. During the months I was there, I had many opportunities to create commercial posters for Eyexpo’s many events. These include poster in an oversea contest, flyers at the night markets, coming soon posters of their new upcoming apps, and graphic assets for the official website.

Each project also had its unique constraints. For example, the contest poster had predetermined text and a very wide cut-off borders while the night market flyers had a lot of content but a very tiny physical format that I had to keep in mind of while designing.

Social Media Banners

For each of their client who requested a customized VR production, a promotional social media banner would be created. If possible, official VR shots taken during production were used to accurately present the project, but if none were found suitable, commercial royalty-free images were edited and photoshopped. Over the course of 5 months, I’ve designed 20+ banners that are up on their official website, Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter.

3D in Unity VR

As Eyexpo expands on their VR capabilities, I was tasked with creating quick environment of their office using Sketchup and their floor plans. Using Sketchup, I modeled the Eyexpo office, placed furniture from the Sketchup 3D warehouse, rendered scenes into normal and 360 panoramic images . Rendered images were then processed in Photoshop to emulate architectural renderings that can be used for future promotions. The environment models I made (office and the convention building) were later exported as FBX and imported into Unity VR for VR simulation. During importing, some furniture’s UVs were flipped, which had to be imported into Maya to be fixed before placed into Unity.

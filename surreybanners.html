Surrey Banners

Winning design for Surrey public street banner contest
The City of Surrey hosted an online contest urging artists, designers and students to submit their proposed design based on the theme of "Water". The winning design was selected from 40 submissions and was up on 150 light poles in the streets of Surrey from 2015 -2016 Spring.

Sketches >> First Draft

During the conceptualizing process, my focus was on developing the theme of "water" into not just the ordinary imagery of a water drop or a pool of water, but how the abundant water resources we have in the Lower Mainland allowed for rich growth in the environment. From this concept, I developed 2 banner designs: "Cycle" and "Bonds".

“Cycle” features two fishes in a pool of water where the two fishes are arranged in a position similar to the symbol of Yin Yang - two opposing forces that gives birth to harmony and coexistence. Specifically, the white fish represents the idea of growth and the orange fish represent the idea of withering and aging. As fishes are a major source of nutrients and income in our particular geography, I feel like it isappropriate to represent them as an important life source that comes with the benefit of having rich water resources.

“Bonds” shows a picture of a tree on top of a still pool of water thatvseems to cascade downwards. I want to show how this natural system of generating fresh water, that includes clouds and lakes, is heavily weaved together with the natural process of life and how everything is connected to each other.

Final Design

The design that won was "Cycle" which went through a few more visual changes. Namely, the two fishes were changed from koi-like physiques to resembling salmon (orange) and whale (white). Additional details and cleaning of the designs were also undertook as well as colour adjustments.

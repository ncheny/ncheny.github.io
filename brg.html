Graphic design intern

Graphic Designs

I worked closely with the digital marketing specialist and the development coordinator in creating and updating graphics for the Gallery. During the term, I was given the task to create graphics for the different programs, exhibitions, and galas that the Gallery would be hosting, as well as promotional ads for the Gallery that were displayed in the Georgia Straight, the Cultch, online Google Ads, Gallery's social media accounts, and other magazines. In addition to digital media, I also designed physical posters were displayed during annual gala, vinyl signs, rack cards at conventions, and entrance digital displays used in the gallery.

Each project usually have different size requirement that the Gallery's printing or publishing partner would have required which I would have to communicate with regularly to check up on file status or to resolve issues that have arisen.

Digital Display

The Gallery has 3 large digital LED screens at the front desk that show guests the admission price, exhibition information, and donor list. I was tasked with updating the existing design to one that is more consistent across the 3 panels and with a visual hierarchy template that can be reused for future exhibitions/or workshops. Over the course of 4 months, I have designed more than 20+ screen displays of workshops and exhibition hosted by the Gallery using the new template. I've also made sure the marketing coordinator would be able to reuse the template by leaving them a design style guide.

Updated Rack Cards

The Gallery have rack cards for their main Gallery, education program, and satellite shops. I was tasked with updating and revamping the older design so that the 3 different rack cards are consistent but distinctive from each other. With the large amount of information that was required to include, size specification, and limitation of the rack card format, where the top of the card need to have the Gallery's name on it, multiple design drafts were made. After a rough proof was accepted by the staff, I contacted the printing press for production. They're displayed at the front desk where Gallery guest can easily grab them, as well as various hotels and conventions.

Social Media Banners

For each of the Gallery's exhibition, community programs, or important events, a promotional social media banner would be created. These banners shares a similar format that can be easily reused for later events. Over the course of 5 months, I’ve designed 30+ banners that are up on their Facebook and Instagram, page.

Styleguide Documentation

At the end of my placement, the marketing specialist requested me to create a styleguide for the next graphic design co-op student and for the new specialist that will be replacing her. Within the last few days, I compiled a documentation of the Gallery's primary and secondary typography that I've used, colour palette that can be paired up, and graphic design heirarchy chart that I've used for the TV screens, social media graphics, and letterhead.

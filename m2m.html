Music 2 Movement

Stroke Rehabilitation Software
  • Our design team collaborated with UBC BEST's (Biomedical Engineering Student Team) Music 2 Movement group to enhance the recovery efforts of stroke patients with the aid of music therapists. Using heuristic evaluation, probes, interviews, questionnaires, and ethnography research, we created a set of interactive user interface for M2M's software.
  • TASKS Create UI mockups, graphic design, schedule interviews, compile data
  • TOOLS Proto.io, Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, Paint Tool SAI
  • TEAM Chris Wong, Joshua Sun, Jonathan Du
  • Fall 2017

Interactive Prototype

What is M2M?

Music 2 Movement (M2M) is part of UBC BEST (Biomedical Engineering Student Team) and is focused on developing muscial therapy devices for stroke rehabilitation patients. An undergrad group of a dozen students from engineering, kinesiology, and computer science, our design team collaborated with M2M to enhance the recovery efforts of stroke patients of musical therapists. Their 3 main research devices monitor progress of patients using musical and motor feedback of patients’ actions.

Goal

M2M's goal is to create assistive music therapy instruments that enhance the recovery process of stroke patients who have suffered damage to their motor functions. Our team felt that it is a good opportunity to gain insight into the pioneering technologies in health science which can help solve real needs of stroke patients.

Ethnography

We visited the M2M group for 3 weeks to conduct ethnographic research by sitting in on their discussion, conducting individual and group interviews with members from the programming, engineering, research group, and the project lead. We also sifted through past Trello boards, Slack conversation, and previous competition documents.

While the M2M team has a robust engineering team and sleek prototype of the therapy devices, the newly formed and understaffed software team frequently gets overwhelmed by their increasing workload.With no defined scope, changing expectations of software, development felt daunting. In addition, the long onboarding period with huge amount of documentation to sift through takes a toll on their time.

Stakeholder Personas

After learning more about M2M, we created 3 personas: computer engineer, music therapist, stroke survivor. Through first hand interviews with the team engineer, a stroke rehab patient, and secondary research, we devised each of their profile, goals,motivations and challenges. The persona we developed, helped us understand each part more and their relationship with each other. However, since direct communication between the M2M team with the patient is near to none, and is mostly with the music therapist, our target audience were narrowed down.

Painpoints Evaluation

We created a set of journey map for the software developer who is working on it and the music therapist who would be using the existing software.For the former, interview data with the 2 main programmers were used to create the journey map, while the latter’s data were collected from our heuristic evaluation of the software. We found that the existing system needs to have more transparent feedback that need to be visible while providing clear indication of further actions, microinteraction to prevent errors, and animation to show progress.

Prototype Concepts

After collecting more data from the M2M group and music therapists through interviews and hand made probe kits, we came to 3 concepts that can improve our personas’ needs. These are an onboarding app for m2m members, interactive display for patients, or a redesign of the current software system geared for less tech savvy music therapists and patients. I created the storyboards needed for each of our concepts, and presented it to M2M.

As we only had limited access to a stroke patient’s caretaker (which meant less data for us to back our designs) and little enthusiasm from the M2M members after presenting the onboarding app to them, our focus shifted towards an interface redesign.

Interface Redesign

By redesigning the interface for the music therapist, it would give us a lot of opportunities to test different design ideas, finetune features, or add a new one.

Using Illustrator, I created a handful of high fidelity UI mockups of the main interface and modal windows. Once all the designs have been approved by the team, we imported it into Proto.io to create interaction animations.

Notable Changes

We presented our redesign of the UI towards the M2M team and compared the old and new design side by side to emphasize the changes and allow the developers to be able to analyze any issues that may arise from the redesign. After a few issues were pointed out and fixed on the spot, we then presented the changes made to the therapist and conducted a more in depth usability study.

Usability Testing

Our final usability test were done with 2 therapists, 1 software developer, and the project lead. The follow-up study consisted of a pre-test questionnaire, tasks to be performed on the new M2M software, post-test questionnaire, and further introspective questions on the tester’s actions.

While we found that there were still some confusions where the system isn’t quite matching to the real world and limited visibility of the system status due to our proto.io prototype, our stakeholders really liked the sleek new look of the M2M software and its emphasis on data efficiency and easiness to use.
